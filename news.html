  1. First anti-neutrino event in T2K

    13th Jun 2014

    On May 26th the first event in anti-neutrino beam mode was observed by the near detector (ND280) of T2K. This is the first ever anti-neutrino beam event in Japan and is the result of the tireless effort of all the members of the T2K collaboration. Moreover, on June 8th 2014, the first fully contained anti-neutrino event was detected in T2K's far detector, Super-Kamiokande. These are the first steps into an exciting time for T2K that will hopefully get us closer to measuring CP violation in the lepton sector.

  2. Neutrino Beam Group recevied SUWA Award

    25th Feb 2014

    The T2K Neutrino Beam group received the SUWA Award for the realization of the world's highest intensity neutrino beam facility, an essential contribution that allowed the discovery of the Electron Neutrino Appearance Phenomenom by the T2K Experiment. Prof. Sampa Bhadra represented the YorkU Neutrino-HEP Group in the award ceremony held on February 17 in Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo. (See also TRIUMF's news report)


    From left to right: Prof. John Martin (UToronto), Chris Densham from Rutherford Appleton Laboratory (UK), Takashi Kobayashi from KEK (Japan), Sampa Bhadra from York University (Canada), Mark Hartz from Kavli IPMU (Japan)/TRIUMF (Canada), and Atsuko Ichikawa from Kyoto University (Japan).

  3. T2K Colloquium at York

    9th Sept 2013

    Prof. Hiro Tanaka from UBC visited us today and presented the status of neutrino physics, as well as the latest results from T2K at the Department of Physics & Astronomy Colloquium. His talk was entitled 'The Era of Three Generations: Possible hints from neutrinos in elucidating the matter/antimatter asymmetry of the Universe'.

  4. NuStorm - Neutrinos from Stored Muons: HEP Seminar

    30th Aug 2013

    Prof. Ken Long from Imperial College London gave a talk on the proposed experiment NuStorm for the HEP Group Seminar. In his talk he explained how neutrinos produced in the decay of stored muon beams have the potential to serve the conventional long-baseline programme, provide definitive measurements of short-baseline oscillations and be developed incrementally to deliver the Neutrino Factory, the facility of choice for the study of neutrino oscillations.

  6. Discovery of neutrino dissapearance by T2K!

    19th July 2013

    New results from T2K were presented today at the European Physical Society meeting in Stockholm, Sweden by Dr. Michael Wilking (TRIUMF). Improvements in all fronts of the analysis and hardware, as well as roughly 4.5 times more data, allow T2K to show conclusively that muon neutrinos transform into electron neutrinos.
