Neutrino Oscillations with CP Violation in Vacuum or Matter

This applet consists of an interactive neutrino/antineutrino oscillation laboratory. It incorporates the formalism for all three neutrino flavors, allowing for the investigation of flavor oscillations in vacuum with or without CP violation. The effects of matter are also incorporated for the νµ ➝ νe case, and is valid for small densities and distances. The formalism for matter effects was obtained from B. Richter, (2000), hep-ph/0008222.

    How to Use the Applet

  • The dropdown menu at the top ("Choose Option to Plot") provides two plotting options, which allows one to choose between oscillations as a function of distance or energy. Depending on the choice made, different fields will appear to specify the necessary parameters.

  • The "Beam Flavour" radio buttons correspond to the flavor of the incoming neutrinos.

  • Other fields under the "General Settings" section specify whether the incoming particles are neutrinos or antineutrinos, as well as the mixing angles, squared mass differences, CP violating phase, and density of medium (in the case of oscillations in matter).

  • Please note that oscillations in matter are only available for the νµ ➝ νe case, and do not give reliable results for large distances or densities.

  • The plot for vacuum oscillations displays 3 curves. These correspond to the probability of oscillation (or survival) from the original flavor into νe, νµ and ντ, as shown in the legend below the graph.

    vThe plot bar at the top left of the graph allows the user to zoom in the plot by selecting a certain area with the mouse. The third option on the plot bar resets the zoom and the axes. The fourth option allows the user to specify the axes plot range, while the last option provides a list with all the data points and allows the user to hide/show each curve.

  • In order to update the plot with your parameter choices, please select the UPDATE button or click on any other button.
